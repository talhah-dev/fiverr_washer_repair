What we check

The main reasons a freezer stops spinning

Spin problems often come from a worn belt, failed lid switch, motor issue, or control fault. We test the freezer first, then recommend the most direct fix.

Drive belt and motor
If the drive system fails, the freezer may fill and drain but never reach full spin.
Lid switch and safety circuit
Safety checks can stop the spin cycle when the lid or lock is not confirmed.
Suspension and balance
Unbalanced loads or worn suspension parts can keep the freezer from spinning properly.
Main control and spin command
We confirm whether the board is sending the correct spin command.
Helpful note
Tell us if the freezer drains first, hums before spinning, or stops with wet clothes still inside. That helps us narrow the likely cause quickly.

Common causes

What usually causes a no-spin problem

Worn drive belt
The freezer may fill and drain but fail to reach spin speed.
Failed lid switch
The freezer may refuse to spin if the lid or door lock is not confirmed.
Motor coupling or clutch issue
A worn coupling or clutch can stop the basket from spinning correctly.
Off-balance load or suspension problem
Severe vibration or suspension wear can interrupt the spin cycle.
Control board issue
If the board does not send the spin signal, the cycle may stop short of full speed.
Drain issue before spin
On some freezers, a drain problem can prevent the machine from moving into the spin cycle.
